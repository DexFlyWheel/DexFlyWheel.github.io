DexFlyWheel: A Scalable and Self-improving Data Generation Framework for Dexterous Manipulation

Highlights

  • DexFlyWheel is the first framework to continuously generate diverse and human-like dexterous manipulation data across different objects, environments, and spatial settings.
  • DexFlyWheel can generate infinite demonstrations with just one human demo per task, significantly reducing human effort.
  • DexFlyWheel exhibits strong generalizability on unseen scenarios in simulation, and successfully transfer in real dual-arm robot system.

Motivation

Existing methods are inherently constrained by their source human demonstrations, suffering from limited diversity when handling different objects. This motivates us to rethink the role of human demonstrations in data generation pipelines. We observe that manipulating different objects only induces minor changes in the manipulation trajectories. This implies that Human demonstrations provide a strong behavioral prior in data generation. Imitation learning (IL) uses it to imitate human-like trajectories. Residual reinforcement learning (RL) further refines these priors to handle diverse objects scenarios.

To achieve generalizable dexterous manipulation in the open world, robots need a mechanism for continuous data collection and policy performance improvement. This inspires us to build a data flywheel that enables continuous self-improvement, where better policies generate more diverse data, and enhanced data leads to more generalizable policies.

Method

Overview of DexFlyWheel. A scalable data generation framework that starts with seed demonstrations warmup and iteratively expands data diversity through a self-improving cycle. Each iteration integrates imitation learning, residual reinforcement learning, rollout trajectory collection, and data augmentation into a closed-loop pipeline.

Experimental Setups

  • To validate the effectiveness of our framework across diverse manipulation scenarios, we designed four challenging tasks encompassing both single-arm and dual-arm dexterous manipulation: grasp, pour, lift, and handover. These tasks cover a wide range of manipulation complexities and coordination demands.
  • For each task, we collected only one human demonstration through a VR-based teleoperation system.
  • We conducted three iterations of data generation within our framework for each task.

Iterative Data Generation Process

Through three iterations of self-improvement, DexFlyWheel achieves:

  • Improvement in policy generalization: from 16.5% to 81.9% success rate on unseen scenarios configurations
  • Increase in data diversity: 214.7× total scenarios with 20× more object variations
  • Enhanced policy performance on various objects test sets: from 48.5% to 82.6% success rate through RL refinement

Performance Scaling with Diverse Datasets Generated by DexFlyWheel

With DexFlyWheel, we achieve simultaneous scaling of both data diversity and policy performance. This indicates the effectiveness of DexFlyWheel in generating diverse datasets, which contribute to enhanced policy generalization.

Comparison with Baselines

Compared to DexMimicGen, our method demonstrates significant advantages in handling diverse objects and executing dynamic tasks, showcasing superior robustness across various manipulation scenarios.

Real Robot Results

Based on the data generated by DexFlyWheel, we successfully deployed it on a dual-arm dexterous robot system.

Contributions

  • We present DexFlyWheel, a novel framework that efficiently generates diverse dexterous manipulation data through iterative self-improving. Starting from a single human demonstrations per task, our framework efficiently generates diversity and high-quality demonstrations while maintaining human-like behavior patterns.
  • We demonstrate the effectiveness of our framework across multiple dexterous manipulation tasks. We generate over 2,000+ successful demonstrations from just one human demonstration per task with 500+ diverse scenarios, including 80 objects, 12 environments and 15 spatial configurations. Our approach significantly outperforms baseline data generation methods in both diversity and robustness.
  • We validate that policies trained with our generated data achieve over average 81.9% success rate in challenging test sets, significantly outperforming policies trained with data from alternative baselines. This superior performance demonstrates the framework's ability to enhance policy generalization across diverse scenarios.
